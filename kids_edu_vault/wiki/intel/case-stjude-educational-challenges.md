# Case — St. Jude Together: Educational Challenges for Pediatric Cancer Patients

St. Jude's patient-facing guide on cognitive/physical side effects and classroom accommodations. **High confidence** — St. Jude is the leading pediatric oncology center.

## Cognitive challenges consistently reported
- Attention, concentration, memory, organization deficits.
- **Slowed processing speed** — writing, note-taking, verbal answering.
- Problem-solving, math, reading, handwriting difficulties.
- Social/vocabulary challenges.

## Physical/sensory
- Hearing/vision impairment from cisplatin, carboplatin chemotherapy.
- Mobility constraints.
- **Reduced energy → limited participation**.

## Accommodations (directly mapped to our pilot)

| St. Jude recommendation | HypeProof pilot equivalent |
|---|---|
| Scribe for writing | **AI as scribe** — child prompts, AI types code. Core premise of [[pilot-env-design]]. |
| Calculator, formula lists | Pre-filled [[single-html-runtime]] starter — no boilerplate typing. |
| Extended time, quiet room | **90분 6블록** with ≤15 min each + ignition/rest between. |
| Seating near front, low traffic | Auditorium layout — small clusters, not theater rows. |
| Frequent check-ins | [[ai-persona-workflows]] W2 Coach + W5 Checklist personas. |
| Avoid unexpected cold-calling | No public Q&A that can embarrass. 1:1 AI interaction. |
| Shortened reading | Korean prompts, visual-first UI, no long text blocks. |
