# Case — "Techquity" in Pediatric/AYA Oncology

Puthenpura et al., 2025, *Pediatric Blood & Cancer*. Framework paper on using AI + immersive tech to **bridge equity gaps** in pediatric, adolescent, and young-adult oncology care.

## Framework
- "Techquity" = leveraging technology to address healthcare disparities.
- Integrates **AI + immersive tech (VR/AR) + internet-enabled devices** as quality-of-life and care-delivery interventions.

## Interventions surveyed
- **AI**: diagnostic accuracy, personalized care plans, patient navigation.
- **Immersive tech**: VR for psychological support, pain management, rehab, **patient education**.
- Internet-enabled devices as gateway to reach underserved populations.

## Equity barriers flagged
- SES, geography, race/ethnicity → unequal access.
- **Digital divide** — not every patient has device/broadband.
- **Ethical considerations** — AI in pediatric care.
- **Cost** barriers to consumer VR/AR.

## Why this matters for HypeProof
- Establishes peer-reviewed **2025 precedent** that AI + immersive tech for pediatric oncology patient-facing use is a legitimate research frontier.
- Our pilot = **patient education via AI** — lands squarely in the Techquity framework.
- **Digital divide caveat is load-bearing**: we provide all devices at venue. This is the only way to bypass the barrier the paper identifies.

## Recommendation for our pilot pitch
Frame HypeProof Kids Edu as a **Techquity intervention**: devices + AI tutor + cooperative narrative = equity-by-design for pediatric oncology patients who otherwise lack AI-tool access.

> [!gap] Paper abstract identifies no specific pediatric coding/AI education case studies. We may be publishable as **one of the first documented implementations** in this space.
