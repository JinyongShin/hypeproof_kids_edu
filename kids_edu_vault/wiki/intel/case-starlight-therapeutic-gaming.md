# Case — Starlight Children's Foundation: Therapeutic Gaming

Large-scale US nonprofit delivering tech-based play to hospitalized children. **Scale precedent**: 2024년 824개 병원 · 300만 환아 임팩트.

## Program mechanic
- Video games, VR, **"therapeutic technology intentionally"** to help kids cope with hospitalization.
- Core mode: **bedside / individual**, not large group.
- Measured outcomes (self-reported by hospital partners):
  - **82%** of partners report Starlight Toy Deliveries reduce stress/anxiety.
  - "Significantly decreased patient anxiety and created a sense of normalcy" (recent study).
  - Framed as gateway to "improved health literacy and better outcomes."

## What transfers to HypeProof
- Tech-as-play is an accepted, evidence-supported modality in pediatric hospitals.
- Framing: **"happiness → trust → engagement → outcomes"** is the causal chain Starlight uses. Our pilot can borrow this narrative for boards/funders.
- Hospital-partner staffing model (Child Life Specialists + volunteers) is the precedent for our **운영자 + 튜터 역할 분리**.

## What does NOT transfer
- Starlight operates **bedside 1:1**, not a 40-child auditorium event. Group dynamics precedent is absent.
- No coding or STEM content — pure play/distraction. We are building a **learning outcome** pilot, which is more ambitious.
- Staffing model assumes paid Child Life Specialists — we will likely run with volunteer tutors.

## Narrative alignment
Starlight explicitly avoids combat/violence framings in hospital contexts. This is **direct external support for [[combat-vs-cooperative-framing]]** — protective, cooperative narratives are the field standard.

> [!gap] Starlight's published outcomes are hospital-partner self-reports, not RCTs. Treat as directional evidence, not causal proof.
